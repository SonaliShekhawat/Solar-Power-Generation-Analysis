2. Temporal Production Trends
Monthly Performance: Energy generation shows a steady upward trend throughout the year. Production is lowest in December (0.50M MW) and peaks in May (0.80M MW), suggesting strong seasonal correlation with longer daylight hours.
Daily Patterns: Generation fluctuates between 0.14M and 0.32M MW daily. There is a noticeable sharp decline toward the end of the month (Day 30), which may warrant investigation into maintenance or weather patterns.
Hourly Distribution: Output follows a classic bell curve. Generation begins around hour 6, reaches a peak of 25 MW at hour 13 (1 PM), and tapers off by hour 20 (8 PM).

3. Performance by Site Type
The data compares Utility-Scale Photovoltaics (UPV) and Distributed Photovoltaics (DPV):
UPV: Exhibits significantly higher capacity (66.67 MW) and average power output (11 MW).
DPV: Operates at a smaller scale with a capacity of 36.85 MW and an average output of 6 MW.
Efficiency: UPV sites are currently the primary drivers of total power generation for this portfolio.
